About Jalgaon Panchayat Samiti

Understanding the role and structure of local governance

What is a Panchayat Samiti?

A Panchayat Samiti is the second tier of the Panchayati Raj system in India, functioning at the block level. It serves as a vital link between Gram Panchayats and Zilla Parishads, coordinating development activities and implementing various government schemes for rural development.

Our Mission

To facilitate sustainable rural development through effective governance, transparent administration, and active community participation, ensuring equitable access to public services and resources.

History & Achievements

Tracing the journey of rural governance in Jalgaon

1959

Establishment of the three-tier Panchayati Raj system in Maharashtra

1962

Formation of Jalgaon Panchayat Samiti to oversee block-level development

1992

Implementation of the 73rd Constitutional Amendment strengthening the Panchayati Raj system

2005

Launch of major rural development initiatives focusing on infrastructure and sanitation

2015

Digital transformation of Panchayat services for improved accessibility

2020

Recognition for exemplary implementation of rural welfare schemes

Services & Responsibilities

Key functions and services provided by the Panchayat Samiti

Administrative Responsibilities

  • Coordination of Gram Sabha activities
  • Rural development planning
  • Budget allocation and management
  • Implementation of government schemes
  • Supervision of Gram Panchayat functioning

Public Welfare Initiatives

  • Rural housing development programs
  • Sanitation and waste management
  • Primary healthcare services
  • Education and literacy programs
  • Women and child welfare schemes

Government Schemes

  • Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana (PMAY)
  • Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA)
  • Digital India initiatives
  • Agricultural assistance programs
  • Rural infrastructure development
